Learn About Phishing (For WhatsApp & Telegram)

This page explains phishing in simple terms for teachers and parents. No technical knowledge needed.

🎭 Scam Pretending
Scammers pretend to be a bank, courier, school, or government.
⚡ Pressure
They rush you: “urgent”, “now”, “last chance”.
🔗 Risky Links
They push you to click a link or open a form.

✅ Quick Checklist (Read This First)

  • Stop: Don’t click links immediately.
  • Check the sender: Is it a real official number/account?
  • Look for pressure: “urgent”, “now”, “account blocked”.
  • Never share OTP/TAC/password even if they sound “official”.
  • Verify using official channels: official website/app (not the message link).

If you’re unsure, ask another teacher/parent or contact the organization using the official number.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a scam where attackers try to steal personal information such as passwords, OTP/TAC codes, bank details, or even your identity by pretending to be someone you trust.

On messaging apps, phishing often looks like a “school update”, “bank warning”, “parcel delivery issue”, or “payment confirmation” — but it is fake.

Common Phishing Scams in Messaging Apps

🏦 Bank / Account Scam

“Account locked. Verify now.” “Suspicious login detected.”

📦 Parcel / Delivery Scam

“Parcel cannot be delivered. Pay delivery fee.”

🎁 Prize / Giveaway Scam

“You won RM500 voucher. Claim now.”

👤 Impersonation Scam

“I’m your friend/teacher. Please transfer money urgently.”

💼 Job / Investment Scam

“Earn RM300/day. Easy job.” “Guaranteed profit.”

🔐 OTP / TAC Scam

“Give OTP to confirm account.” (Real services don’t ask this.)

🚩 Red Flags (Warning Signs)

  • Urgent / pressure words: “cepat”, “urgent”, “sekarang”, “last chance”.
  • Asking for sensitive info: OTP/TAC, password, bank account number.
  • Strange link: short links (bit.ly), weird domains, many symbols, unusual endings (.xyz).
  • Too good to be true: big rewards, gifts, huge discounts.
  • Sender doesn’t match: “bank” message from random phone number or free email.
  • Spelling/grammar issues or unnatural mixed language can appear in scams.

🛡️ What You Should Do

  1. Do not click links or download files.
  2. Do not share OTP/TAC/password.
  3. Verify using official app/website (type it yourself, don’t tap link).
  4. Ask for confirmation (e.g., school office / bank hotline).
  5. Report the scam if possible.

More Details (Optional)

Why Scammers Use Links

Scam links often open fake login pages that look real. If you type your password or OTP there, scammers can steal your account or money.

What is a “Domain” (Website Name)?

A domain is the website name like maybank2u.com.my or moe.gov.my. Scammers often use similar names like maybank2u-login.xyz to trick victims.
